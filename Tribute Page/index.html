Mustafa Kemal Atatürk

The founder of modern Turkey

Here's a timeline of Mustafa Kemal Atatürk's life:

  • 1881 - Born in Salonika (modern-day Thessaloniki, Greece), part of the Ottoman Empire.
  • 1893 - Enters the military academy in Istanbul, where he shows great promise as a student and military leader.
  • 1905 - Graduates as a lieutenant and joins the Ottoman Army, where he begins his long military career.
  • 1915 - Becomes a national hero during the Battle of Gallipoli, defending the Ottoman Empire from Allied forces.
  • 1919 - Launches the Turkish War of Independence after the Ottoman Empire’s defeat in World War I, rejecting the harsh terms of the Treaty of Sèvres.
  • 1923 - Establishes the Republic of Turkey, abolishing the Ottoman Sultanate and becoming the nation’s first president.
  • 1924 - Abolishes the Caliphate, separating religion from government and embarking on a wide range of reforms to modernize Turkey.
  • 1934 - Receives the surname “Atatürk,” meaning “Father of the Turks,” in recognition of his immense contributions to the Turkish nation.
  • 1938 - Passes away on November 10 at the age of 57, leaving a legacy as one of the most transformative leaders of the 20th century.

"Atatürk’s reforms changed Turkey from a religious, multi-ethnic empire into a secular, nationalist state. His vision transformed a struggling post-Ottoman country into a modern nation."

-- BBC News
